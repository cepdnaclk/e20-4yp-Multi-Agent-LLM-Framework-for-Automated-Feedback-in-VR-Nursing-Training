HISTORY TAKING GUIDELINES
Post-Operative Wound Care

PURPOSE

To gather essential patient information before performing wound care in order to ensure patient safety, correct clinical decisions, and effective communication.

1. INTRODUCTION & PATIENT IDENTIFICATION (MANDATORY)

The student must introduce themselves and verify patient identity before any procedure.

Required Questions:

“Good morning, I am a nursing student…”

“May I know your name, please?”

“Your age, please?”

“Your address, please?”

Why This Is Important:

Prevents treating the wrong patient

Establishes professional rapport

Required for legal and clinical documentation

2. PROCEDURE EXPLANATION & COMMUNICATION

The student should clearly explain:

What procedure will be performed

Why it is needed

Use simple, non-technical language

Example:

“The doctor has prescribed cleaning your wound and applying a dressing to help it heal.”

Why This Is Important:

Reduces patient anxiety

Improves cooperation and trust

3. ALLERGY ASSESSMENT (CRITICAL SAFETY STEP)

The student must ask about allergies before wound care.

Required Question:

“Do you have any history of allergy to medications or plasters?”

What to Identify:

Medication allergies (e.g., penicillin)

Dressing, tape, or latex allergies

Why This Is Important:

Prevents allergic reactions

Directly affects dressing and solution choice

4. PAIN ASSESSMENT

Pain should be assessed before starting the procedure.

Required Question:

“Do you feel any pain at the wound site?”

Expected Information:

Pain presence

Pain severity (mild, moderate, severe)

Why This Is Important:

Ensures patient comfort

Severe pain may indicate complications

5. BASIC NEEDS ASSESSMENT

The student should ensure the patient is comfortable before beginning.

Required Question:

“Do you need to go to the bathroom or is there anything you need before we start?”

Why This Is Important:

Prevents interruption during procedure

Demonstrates patient-centered care

6. PROFESSIONAL COMMUNICATION PRINCIPLES
DO:

✔ Introduce yourself
✔ Speak clearly and politely
✔ Explain each step
✔ Ask if the patient is comfortable

DO NOT:

✘ Rush the patient
✘ Assume information
✘ Ignore patient concerns

7. SUMMARY CHECKLIST (EVALUATION BASIS)

Before proceeding, the student should have:

☑ Introduced themselves
☑ Verified patient identity
☑ Explained the procedure
☑ Asked about allergies
☑ Assessed pain
☑ Checked basic needs

Failure to complete these steps should be flagged by evaluator agents.

KEY PRINCIPLES

Patient safety comes first

Clear communication improves care quality

All findings must be based on patient responses

If it is not asked, it is considered not done

END OF GUIDELINES
